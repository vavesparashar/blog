Yes. You may know multiple areas, but know what you're an expert at and what you're not.

Too often people want to act like they know every area of digital marketing and they see $$ so they don't want to tell the client "We don't really know that area." So they fake it and end up screwing themselves and their clients when they fail to deliver on something they weren't actually familiar with. In the end **it hurts them, it hurts the client, and it hurts all of us because they don't trust marketers.**

I worked for an agency that would do such. After I left, they had a client they did content work for, that mentioned they were looking someone to manage their ads on a particular platform (not a common one). Their president claimed they knew it well, and got the client to sign a contract to have them manage it. They then had one of their managers read everything he could on the platform over the weekend. Within a week it was clear to the client they had no idea how to manage the platform and had lied about their abilities. Not only did they fire the agency, but it cost them potential business with other clients. This was a Fortune 500 company, and employees from such move around to other Fortune 500s frequently and communicate their experience with previous agencies.
